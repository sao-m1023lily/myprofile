I'm Saori Morita. I am broadly interested in human language and the neural mechanisms that support its processing. My research centers on language impairments arising from conditions such as aphasia and psychiatric symptoms, using approaches that integrate large language models and machine learning. Having personally experienced a period of illness of unknown origin—and the uncertainty that came with it—I carry that experience as a source of motivation for my work. Through my day-to-day research, I aim to advance, step by step, our understanding of natural language—shaped by many interacting factors—and, in parallel, our understanding of language disorders, which are likewise multifactorial.
